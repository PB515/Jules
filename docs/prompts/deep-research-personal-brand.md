# Prompt — Deep Research (Personal Brand & Portfolio)

*Toolkit prompt · feeds SOP Step 2a (personal-brand path) · output feeds doc generation (Step 3)*

**When to use this one.** For a **personal brand, portfolio, founder, creator, or consultant** site — where the site wins by being *authentically this person*, not by beating a market. For a **business / product / service** with competitors (CA firm, e-commerce, SaaS, landing page, local services), use `deep-research-business.md` instead. Tie-breaker: *does this site win by being better than competitors, or by being authentically this person?*

This prompt studies **inspirations, not competitors** — there is no market gap to find, there's a positioning space to own.

---

## How to use

Fill these fields, paste the prompt below into a capable AI (web access strongly preferred), and let it run:

```
PERSON NAME:          [e.g. Purven Bhavsar]
CURRENT POSITIONING:  [e.g. AI Automation Engineer]
FUTURE DIRECTION:     [e.g. Systems thinking + infrastructure]
AUDIENCE:             [e.g. recruiters, founders, peers, mentors]
PURPOSE OF SITE:      [e.g. build a personal brand, showcase work, publish writing]
```

Then run the **GATE** (below) before trusting the output. The output feeds the Business Brief, the PRD, doc 03b (site map), doc 04 (design system), and your positioning.

---

## THE PROMPT (copy from here)

```
You are a senior personal-branding strategist, content strategist, and design analyst.

Your task is NOT to study competitors. Your task is to study INSPIRATIONS — strong
personal brands, premium design, and great writing — to help design a memorable,
authentic personal-brand website that communicates who this person is, how they think,
what they've built, and where they're going.

Optimise for authenticity, clarity, memorability, and long-term brand value — never
self-promotion or buzzwords. Use real, named examples with links, and back observations
with specifics (a quote, a layout detail), not generic advice. If you don't know
something about the person, write "TBD" — do NOT invent facts, projects, or credentials.

Produce the following:

1. PERSONAL-BRAND INSPIRATIONS — 5 strong personal brands (founders, thinkers, builders,
   writers). For each: link · what makes it memorable · voice / storytelling · what to
   BORROW · what to AVOID.

2. DESIGN INSPIRATIONS — 5 premium sites (e.g. Anthropic, Stripe, Linear, Vercel). For
   each: visual style · typography · layout · colour · navigation · trust technique;
   then what to BORROW / AVOID.

3. WRITING / FIELD-NOTES INSPIRATIONS — 5 writing-first sites (essays, blogs, digital
   gardens). For each: content structure · readability · voice · how knowledge is
   organised; then BORROW / AVOID.

4. PORTFOLIO INSPIRATIONS — 5 exceptional portfolios. For each: homepage structure ·
   how projects/case studies are presented · visual hierarchy · trust signals; then
   BORROW / AVOID.

5. PATTERN SYNTHESIS — across all inspirations, the recurring patterns for: hero
   sections · navigation · project presentation · about pages · resume · contact ·
   content hierarchy · and how strong brands tell their story (origin → current work →
   future direction → philosophy). Output a recommended storytelling framework.

6. TRUST-BUILDING — how identity-led sites earn credibility (experience, work, writing,
   consistency, transparency, public learning, community). Output a recommended trust
   strategy for this person.

7. POSITIONING & DIFFERENTIATOR — THE MOST IMPORTANT SECTION. Based on everything above,
   name the specific, underserved space this personal brand can own. Then complete:
   "This site stands apart because ___" in one sentence that connects the person's
   STORY + WORK + VISION.
   This must be SPECIFIC, DEFENSIBLE, and AUTHENTIC. Reject generic non-answers — a
   bare "bridges X and Y" or "passionate about Z" does not count. Push for the angle
   only this person can credibly hold, and say plainly what makes it defensible.

8. MVP SITE STRUCTURE — recommend the navigation and the section order for each page
   (Home, About, Portfolio, Field Notes, Contact, and where the resume lives), and
   explain WHY. (This feeds the site map, doc 03b.)

9. CONTENT STRATEGY (phased) — what content ships at MVP, Phase 2, and Phase 3, for
   each page type, with the priority order and the reasoning.

10. VISUAL DIRECTION (directional, not final) — the visual MOOD, the colour direction,
    the type direction, and the photography / iconography / illustration approach, with
    reasoning. Give this as DIRECTION to inform the design system — do NOT lock final
    colour values here; doc 04 sets the actual tokens and verifies WCAG contrast.

11. PHASED ROADMAP — MVP / Phase 2 / Phase 3: for each, what to build, why it matters,
    and relative complexity.

END WITH A ONE-PAGE STRATEGIC SUMMARY: the strongest positioning, the strongest visual
and storytelling direction, the top things to borrow, and the single clearest way this
personal brand can become memorable.

Here are the inputs:
PERSON NAME: [ ]
CURRENT POSITIONING: [ ]
FUTURE DIRECTION: [ ]
AUDIENCE: [ ]
PURPOSE OF SITE: [ ]
```

---

## GATE (check before trusting the output)

```
[ ] Studies INSPIRATIONS, not competitors — with specific named sites + observations
[ ] The positioning gap is SPECIFIC and defensible — NOT a generic "bridges X and Y" restatement
[ ] Has a clear one-sentence differentiator connecting story + work + vision
[ ] Borrow-vs-avoid is concrete (a detail or quote), not "have a clear CTA"
[ ] Produces a complete MVP site structure (maps to doc 03b) + a phased roadmap
[ ] Visual direction is DIRECTIONAL (mood/feel) — final tokens + contrast left to doc 04
[ ] Nothing about the person is invented — unknowns are "TBD"
```

If the differentiator is soft or any box fails, send it back and make it sharper. The differentiator is the personal-brand equivalent of the business path's killer feature — a vague one produces a forgettable site.

---

*Inspirations, not competitors. A personal-brand site wins on an authentic, defensible positioning — find the space only this person can own, and let the design and writing express it. One source of truth.*
